Assignment: Part 2: Formula Calculator
Purpose: After the user inputs the value for the coefficients a,b, and c, the program puts the real solutions for the quadratic equation ax^2+bx+c=0
Explaining solution:
I used the formula for the discriminant to find the solutions (D = b^2-4ac).
When the discriminant is above 0, there are 2 real solutions. x1=(-b+sqrt(D))/(2a),x2=(-b-sqrt(D))/(2a)
When the discriminant is equal to 0, there are 2 equivalent real solutions. x=(-b)/(2a)
When the discriminant is less than 0, there are no real solutions.

x: -1.000000. (correct)
